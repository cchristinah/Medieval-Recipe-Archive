Apple Pie

Recipe translated from The Forme of Cury, c. 1390

“For to make Tartys in Applis. Tak gode Applys and gode Spycis and Figys and reysons and Perys and wan they are wel ybrayed coloured with Safron well and do yt in a cofyn and yt forth to bake wel.” — The Forme of Cury c. 1390

Ingredients

Filling

  • Saffron
  • Water
  • Apples
  • Pears
  • Raisins
  • Figs

Crust

  • Water
  • Suet or Lard
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Egg

Instructions

For the filling:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Soak saffron in water to create a saffron water.
  3. Core and chop apples and pears.
  4. Mash apples and pears in a mortar and pestle until mushy.
  5. Place the mixture into a bowl. Strain saffrons out of the water and mix into the mixture with raisins and chopped figs.
  6. Mix together surgas and spices, then combine with fruit mix.

For the crust:

  1. Boil water. Stir in the suet/lard and salt. Let fat melt.
  2. Place flour in a large bowl. Once mixture is simmering, pour into the flour and stir together. Once it is cool enough to handle, knead the dough until smooth.
  3. Roll out the dough and place into a large disc.
  4. Place the filling in the center of the dough disc and fold over to form a pie crust.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes until crust is golden brown.
